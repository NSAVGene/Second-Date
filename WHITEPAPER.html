How the Profile Works

Not a questionnaire. Behavioral inference.

The AI interview is built on a principle borrowed from clinical psychology and professional matchmaking: past behavior predicts future compatibility far better than stated preferences. People don't always know what they want — but they always know what they've experienced. So we ask about that instead.

Every question is behavioral. "Tell me about the last time a conversation left you feeling energized" reveals social style, emotional attunement, and connection needs in a single answer. No checkbox captures that. A skilled listener does.

The categories the AI covers map to established psychological frameworks:

Attachment Style

How they respond when someone they like goes quiet. What changes about them when they're genuinely interested. Whether they move fast or slow — and what drives that.

Social Energy

Whether people fill them up or drain them. How much alone time they actually need. The difference between who they are at a party and who they are one-on-one.

Behavioral Consistency

Do they show up the same way regardless of who they're with — or do they adapt? This dimension is invisible on a profile. In conversation, it surfaces immediately.

Implicit Dealbreakers

Things people say don't matter — but do. Things they didn't know were non-negotiable until they were missing. These are never declared. They're inferred from what someone keeps circling back to.

Self-Awareness

What they've learned about themselves from past patterns. Whether they can name what they ignore early on. Self-awareness predicts whether someone can actually sustain a connection — not just start one.

Date Style & Pace

Real preferences, not aspirational ones. Low-key by default or willing to step it up? Fast burn or slow burn? These are different people who should be matched with their own kind.

After the conversation, the AI synthesizes everything it heard — not through a scoring algorithm, but through the same pattern recognition a matchmaker or therapist applies after a first session. It identifies what the person showed, not just what they said. The archetype name ("The Self-Contained Realist," "The Slow Burn Optimist") is a human-readable summary of a multidimensional behavioral picture.

The profile gets sharper over time. Post Date Review data feeds real-world outcomes back into the system — what predicted connection, what didn't, what the gap map reveals. The interview is the starting point. The dates are the training data.

The Behavioral Drift Index (BDI)

After a user completes their interview and receives their profile, the app keeps the conversation open. They can add context, soften an answer, change something, or go deeper on a topic. These post-profile revisions are not corrections — they are the most valuable behavioral signal in the entire system.

What someone says under questioning is one data point. What they feel compelled to revisit after seeing themselves reflected back is another. The delta between the two — the drift — reveals emotional salience that no direct question can surface.

The BDI tracks the direction, topic, and intensity of every revision. It is weighted heavily in profile generation and matching, and is never surfaced to the user. People who know they're being measured perform. The BDI only works because they don't.

Softening an answer

A user who describes an ex harshly, then goes back to add nuance, is still processing. Match with someone patient, not someone intense.

Intensifying a dealbreaker

A user who adds "actually that's a hard no for me" to something they initially downplayed got burned by it. Weight accordingly.

Returning to a topic unprompted

If they bring it up again without being asked, it matters more than anything they said when they were asked.

Not revising at all

High self-awareness. What they said first is what they meant. Low edit count correlates with emotional clarity — itself a matching signal.

No competitor can replicate BDI without fundamentally redesigning their product. It requires a post-profile edit layer, a tracking architecture, and a profile generator that weights revision history as distinct signal. It cannot be bolted onto an existing app. It has to be built in from the start.

Safety Architecture

The only app where you cannot reach the first date without verifying the person is real.

Safety is not a feature we added. Like privacy, it is structural. Two systems work in parallel — one before the date, one during it.

🎥

Your First Second Date

Before the venue unlocks, both parties complete a short live in-app video call. We call this your First Date. When you meet in person, it is technically your second — and it feels that way. The stranger energy is gone. The ice is already broken. You are picking up a conversation, not starting one.

After the call ends, the app presents a single screen: "Congratulations. You just had your first date. Now let's plan the second one." The venue suggestions unlock. The booking appears. The safety check is invisible because the framing is entirely about excitement.

Catfish can fabricate photos and steal identities. They cannot fake a live face-to-face call. Zero strangers. Guaranteed.

🛡

Safety Date Widget

When a date is confirmed, the Safety Widget activates. It logs who you are meeting, their profile, photo, and the booked venue. A trusted contact is notified automatically. Live location is tracked and compared against the expected venue — if either party leaves the venue unexpectedly or arrives somewhere off-plan, the app sends a check-in nudge immediately. If location moves to an unplanned residential address, the alert fires without waiting for the hourly interval.

Every hour, both phones receive a silent full-screen prompt: one large green button, one large red button. No text needed. Green resets the timer. Red dispatches the trusted contact instantly with full date details, location, and profile. No response within 10 minutes is treated as red. Silence is the trigger.

A pattern of non-responses on one party's profile is logged quietly by the AI as a behavioral signal.

Because the app already knows the booked venue, every location signal is meaningful. The app doesn't guess where you should be. It knows.
